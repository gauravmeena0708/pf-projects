EPFO: Integrating Knowledge Base into Policy Cycle

A comprehensive knowledge base enhances every stage of the policy cycle:

  • Agenda Setting: Identify emerging issues and trends from integrated data that require policy attention.
  • Policy Formulation: Use the knowledge base to understand potential impacts, target populations, and resource requirements for different policy options.
  • Decision Making: Provide policymakers with robust, evidence-based scenarios.
  • Implementation Planning: Leverage insights on member segments and regional variations for effective rollout.
  • Monitoring & Evaluation: Continuously update the knowledge base with new data to track policy performance and conduct rigorous causal evaluations.

Research Frontiers in Knowledge Base Construction for Policy

Building and utilizing such knowledge bases offers rich research avenues:

  • Automated Knowledge Base Population: Techniques to automatically extract information and relationships from unstructured policy documents and news.
  • Temporal Knowledge Graphs: Representing how relationships and policy impacts evolve over time.
  • Explainable AI for Policy Insights: Making the reasoning behind AI-driven policy recommendations transparent and understandable.
  • Integrating Qualitative Data: Methods to incorporate insights from surveys, interviews, and expert opinions into the quantitative knowledge base.
  • Cross-Domain Knowledge Transfer: Applying learnings from one policy area or social security scheme to another.
